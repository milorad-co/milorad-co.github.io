Section b. military

0) Individual generals of ground forces propose actions to the Leaders of Peteland. If both Leaders agree to take an action, the general may order their troops to take it. Otherwise, the action is not taken. The Leaders may also command generals to take actions.
1) The Leader of Air and Naval Forces recives and sends military actions and information similarly to the Leaders. However, the Leader of Air and Naval Forces must gain the permissiion of both Leaders before taking or approving an action.
2) At any time, if both Leaders agree on it, a State of Military Emergency can be declared. During a State of Military Emergency, all generals, as well as the Leader of Air and Naval forces, do not need permission from the Leaders to take military actions.
